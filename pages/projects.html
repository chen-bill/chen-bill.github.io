Alexa Keep

February 27th, 2016

About

A skill for the Amazon Alexa that allows users to store any information they might want to know in the future.
For example,

-"When is my mother’s birthday?"
-"When did I last replace my air filter?"
-"What’s my drivers license number?"

Alexa Keep needs to be taught any information you want it to remember. For example,
-"My mother's birthday is Feb 20, 1960"
-"I replaced my air filter yesterday"
-"My drivers license number is 1234567890"

Alexa Keep can differentiate between what type of data (What, Where, When) the user says and maps appropriate commands to retreive this data.
-"When is my mothers birthday" vs "What is my mother's birthday"
-"What is my driver license number" vs "Where is my drivers license number"

Sous Chef

February 27th, 2016

About

Sous Chef is a personal voice activated kitchen assitance that provides users with on demand recipe instructions, ingredients, and other essential cooking capabilities. By hooking up BigOven's recipe API with the Amazon Echo, we give uses access to over 350,000 recipes with just one vibration of the vocal chord. Sous Chef comes with a Nodejs webapp which gives users the ability to search for recipes and add them to their favourites, which can be later accessed through the Echo.

Jarvis

September 18th - September 20th, 2015

About

I've been struggling with receiving phone notifications and navigation data while riding my motorcycle. Oftentimes I miss my turns due to my lack of navigational skills without a GPS, and miss important calls and messages due to the phone's ringer and vibration being nullified by the environment.

We created a Hybrid application which sends navigational information from the Google Maps API (maneuvers), as well as other notification data (sms, phone calls) to the Arduino. The Arduino takes this data and manipulates the array of 8 LEDs to display information to the user in an intuitive way. The lights rely strictly on peripheral vision, allowing the user to be notified of events without taking his/her eyes of the road.

FANCAR 2.0

November 2014 - June 2015

About

An electric longboard powered by a single RC motor attached to an Electric Speed Controller which is controlled by an Arduino. The Arduino has a bluetooth module that enables Bluetooth LE pairing with an Android application. We programmed the Android application to relay inputs from a paired Pebble smartwatch to the bluetooth module which controls the Arduino’s outputs to the Electric Speed Controller. By doing this, we are able to control the speed and direction of the longboard at the convience of our wrist.

Media

Demo1 Demo2 Demo3 Demo4

Background

I’ve been longboarding for over four years. My first board was a Sector 9 pintail that I bought some time during the summer of 2011. About a year later, I added another board to my quiver, the Rayne Rival, a sweet bamboo/fiberglass board that I used for sliding and free riding (though the constant bruising and scrapes eventually drove me away from doing longboarding tricks). I threw in a random Churchill MFG penny board for the kicks and giggles after marveling at my friend Phillip’s insane penny-boarding skills. Never would I even think of slapping a motor onto a longboard, until I stumbled upon Boosted Board’s kickstarter campaign.

The Boosted Dual, a two wheeled electricity powered longboard with over 7 miles of range, top speed of 20mph, weighing in at 15 pounds: $1299 + Tax and Shipping.
-Boosted Boards

Damn. That’s all of my OSAP money for the next two terms. That’s more than 260 reggae kitchen meals. Guess it’s time for a DIY.

And thus, the FANCAR 2.0 (Fast And Nimble Charge Actuated Roadster) was born. Named after the infamous fan powered car that a curious Waterloo Physics student from the 1980s, 1970’s, 1900’s built to make the commute from his home to campus more convenient (but failed after realizing what the conservation of momentum was).

NOtorcycle

September 17th, 2015

About

This watchface pulls the user's geolocation and loops through a weather API with hourly precipitation data. After analyzing at the precipitation probability and the precipitation intensity for the next 12 hours, the app outputs hours remaining until rainfall (“Dry next: #h”) and hours remaining until it rains too much to ride a bike safely (“Bikeable next: #h”).

A convenient way to check for specific weather information. Also acts as a reminder to wear your pebble watch while motorcycling (to get messaging/phone notifications).
